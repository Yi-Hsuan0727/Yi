Overview

Web-first AI bill splitter—scan a receipt, split fairly, and share in under two minutes, no app install.

01 — Problem Statement

Group checks break on math, OCR, and tax or tip

Splitting U.S. receipts with tax and tip is slow and error-prone—existing apps feel too simple or too unreliable to trust at the table.

Calculation fatigue

Manual splits often miss by pennies—and that erodes trust.

Low OCR trust

Bad scans take longer to fix than typing the bill by hand.

Tax & tip confusion

Percent vs. fixed add-ons are rarely explained clearly.

02 — Project Goals

Two goals: fast splits, trusted math

Success meant a frictionless user flow and a web MVP that finishes in under two minutes.

User Goal

Scan, assign items, and share the split instantly.

Business / Product Goal

Ship a web-first MVP with sessions under 2 minutes.

03 — Key Solutions

Smart assignment, transparent math, and instant web sharing

Five core features work together to eliminate every friction point — from login to the final shared link.

Solution 01

Frictionless Google Authentication

One-tap login stores your splitting history safely without tedious onboarding forms.

Solution 02

Instant OCR Side-by-Side Review

Users snap or upload a photo, and the AI extracts items, prices, tax, and tip instantly. The UI places this data alongside the receipt photo for quick confirmation.

Solution 03

Tap-to-Split Multi-Selection

Tap an item, select the clean text tags showing the names of the members who shared it, and watch the app divide the cost dynamically and equally.

Solution 04

Proportional Tax & Tip Engine

The backend accurately distributes tax and tip proportionally based only on what each individual actually consumed.

Solution 05

Web-First Real-Time Sharing

Share a clean web link so friends can see exactly what they owe—no app download required.

Phase 02 — Interaction Design

Core Flow Iteration

Pic2Split goal: under 2 minutes per session.

Google SSO, name tags for dishes, and verify-OCR-first flow replaced competitors' scan → confusing tax or tip → number wall — cutting time from 4–5 min to under 2 for the product we built.

Phase 03 — Wireframing

23-Screen Architecture

23 screens—each with a job, none decorative.

23 wireframes covered happy paths, errors, same-person pay, and side-by-side checks on every money step. The component library—buttons, inputs, tags, and reusable patterns—was built on Tailwind CSS, so screens shared one token-based system ready for build.

05 — Reflection

Technology alone doesn't solve user problems — trust and transparency do

Building an AI-driven product taught me that technology alone doesn't solve user problems — trust and transparency do. By evaluating our competitors, we learned that when AI isn't 100% transparent, users feel out of control. Designing fallback mechanisms, like manual editing and side-by-side reviews, built that trust.
