Introduction

Overview

The United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) works globally to protect cultural heritage, advance education, and support sustainability. However, despite its monumental real-world impact, many potential volunteers and advocates struggle to navigate the UNESCO website to find relevant opportunities.

This project focused on identifying usability barriers and redesigning the UNESCO digital experience to connect passionate individuals with actionable engagement opportunities.

Problem Statement

Usability barriers blocking engagement

Through an initial heuristic evaluation and user research, it became clear that the UNESCO website suffered from content discoverability issues, navigation inconsistencies, and unclear role descriptions.

Potential volunteers and professionals encountered:

Hidden career pathways

Difficulty locating the Career and Volunteering search sections from the homepage.

Loading without feedback

Delays in page loading without visual feedback, causing frustration.

Confusing filters

Confusing categorizations and overlapping filter functionalities on the job board.

Text-heavy listings

Large blocks of text without proper hierarchy, making it hard to understand internship and volunteer requirements.

Project Goals

Guiding objectives for the redesign

Improve Discoverability

Create clear, accessible pathways to NGO partnerships, volunteering opportunities, and student programs.

Enhance Clarity

Redesign job and volunteer listings to clearly define responsibilities, required skills, and expected duration.

Streamline Navigation

Fix redundant filters and provide intuitive viewing options (Map, List, and Gallery views) for career searching.

Increase Engagement

Optimize the website's structure to reduce user frustration and drive higher participation in UNESCO's initiatives.

01 — Research & Discovery

UNESCO Website User Experience Survey

I began by launching the UNESCO Website User Experience Survey to understand the demographics, goals, and pain points of potential users. The survey gathered qualitative and quantitative data from 10 respondents.

Key Findings

Unclear navigation

Users faced unclear navigation as a major challenge.

Lack of engagement options

Users struggled to find actionable ways to get involved.

Time constraints

Time constraints added friction to exploring opportunities.

Motivation

Despite hurdles, users are highly motivated by career development, networking, and a desire to make a tangible impact.

Design implication

The redesign needed to shorten paths to opportunity while respecting users' limited time.

02 — Empathizing with the User

Personas

Based on the research, I developed three distinct personas to guide my design decisions:

The Curious Student

Li Wei, 22

Lacks knowledge on how to get involved and needs clear onboarding and introductory programs.

The Cultural Advocate

Aarav Mehta, 35

Looking for long-term sustainable impact but struggles with poor website accessibility and unclear NGO transparency.

The Impact Innovator

Alex Chen, 29

A busy UX Designer needing short-term, structured, skill-based volunteering roles.

03 — Interface Audit & Heuristic Evaluation

Career Portal blockers

I conducted an Interface Audit focusing heavily on the Career Portal, which presented the biggest blockers.

01

Buried career search

The original site buried the career search section, requiring users to leave the main site.

02

Mismatched filters

Filter categories did not match the job list, and text-heavy formatting made scanning difficult.

04 — Ideation & Wireframing

Restructuring information architecture

To solve these issues, I restructured the information architecture. My primary focus was surfacing the search section directly on the home page and overhauling the job filtering system.

Decision

I conceptualized a redesigned search bar placed prominently at the top of the page, allowing users to find jobs with just one click.

Decision

I planned to introduce application statistics, featured jobs, and job alerts to help users track opportunities.

05 — Testing & Iteration

Usability Test Plan Dashboard

I created a Usability Test Plan Dashboard to evaluate the redesign. I designed a script and observation sheet to test three core scenarios matching my personas:

  • Finding NGO Partnerships
  • Locating Short-term Volunteering
  • Student Engagement

By testing these user flows, I identified exactly where users hesitated. I iterated on the visual feedback, adding loading indicators to reduce perceived wait times, and refined the interactive cues (like hover effects) that were previously missing.

Key Solutions

Final design

The final high-fidelity designs address the core usability issues while elevating the visual experience to match UNESCO's prestigious global brand.

Centralized Career Search

The career portal is now directly accessible, featuring a streamlined search bar, application statistics, and job alerts.

Advanced Filtering & Views

I improved the career search filters to accurately categorize opportunities by role type. Users can toggle between Map View, List View, and Gallery View.

Structured Job Listings

Internship and volunteer pages clearly define responsibilities, required skills, and duration using a scannable, hierarchical format.

Performance Feedback

Implemented visual loading indicators to enhance the perception of site performance and reduce user drop-off.

Reflection

Balancing scale with focused user needs

Designing for an organization as expansive as UNESCO required balancing vast amounts of information with the specific, focused needs of individual users.

The most challenging aspect was resolving the overlapping filter categories on the original job board. By stepping back and analyzing the data through the lens of my personas—Aarav, Alex, and Li Wei—I was able to create a structured taxonomy that made sense for different levels of commitment.

Summary / Impact

Vision, problems solved, and goals achieved

Vision

To make volunteering and engagement with UNESCO accessible, clear, and inspiring.

Problems Solved

Eliminated navigation dead-ends, structured text-heavy job descriptions into scannable formats, and fixed redundant search filters.

Goals Achieved

Delivered a streamlined, user-tested interface that guides students to entry-level roles, connects professionals with skill-based tasks, and helps advocates find long-term partnerships effortlessly.

Ultimately, this project proves that making engagement opportunities easier to discover directly increases a user's willingness to participate and volunteer.
