Longtan Walker

Overview

Walk to Win is a behavior-driven mobility concept that encourages residents and students in Longtan to walk short trips instead of relying on motorcycles. By combining step tracking with local discounts, the app turns everyday walking into a practical, rewarding habit.

The Problem

A Habit of Riding Short Distances

Field observations around Longtan Road during peak meal hours showed that many students and residents defaulted to motorcycles, even for walkable distances.

🛵 Heavy Traffic

High concentration of motorcycles during school and meal rushes.

🅿️ Limited Space

Tight parking and crowded sidewalks at key stretches.

🫁 Air & Safety

Poor air quality and lower pedestrian safety at crossings.

The Solution

Make Walking Rewarding

Longtan Walker turns steps into redeemable value. Users walk, earn a virtual currency, and redeem rewards at local stores—aligning personal health with neighborhood impact.

Design Process

Why People Didn’t Walk

Surveys and interviews revealed that distance wasn’t the barrier. The real issue was a lack of immediate incentive, while local businesses clearly wanted more foot traffic.

This insight shaped three core features that connect walking behavior with social motivation and local rewards.

Core Features

Walk → Earn → Spend Locally

1. Step to Wealth (Pedometer & Tracking)

  • Real-time conversion: Daily step counts are visualized as a monetary-like value.
  • Data visualization: The main page shows daily steps, accumulated amount, and a simple trend chart for weekly comparison.

2. Gamified Competition Mode

  • Leaderboards: Weekly and monthly rankings encourage friendly competition.
  • Social proof: Seeing peers succeed nudges users to increase their daily steps.

3. Local Reward Redemption

The incentive: Stores along Longtan Road offer exclusive discounts on food and beverages.

The payoff: Users redeem products using accumulated step value, directly driving foot traffic to local storefronts.

Summary

What I Learned

Problems solved

Reduced short-distance vehicle trips on Longtan Road by offering a clear alternative: walk instead of ride.

Goal achieved

Translated abstract issues—parking, air quality, congestion—into a concrete loop: Walk → Earn → Spend Locally.

The vision

A city where health and local economy reinforce each other: better mobility habits that are good for both people and the neighborhood.
